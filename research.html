Dissertation

As Deep Neural Networks(DNNs) are broadly applied in different domains, their robustness problem becomes prominent. In order to estimate the robustness of neural networks(NN), Adversarial Examples are generated to find its upper bound. However, the upper bound cannot guarantee the non-existence of adversarial examples for a DNN on an input space. Hence the community is now paying more attention to estimation of the lower bound of robustness for neural networks, due to the fact that the it can guarantee that there is no adversarial examples within the input space quantified by the estimated lowerbound. In this paper, we make two significant contributions. Firstly, we extend the robustness metric CLEVER to amore general model which is based on Generalized Extreme Value(GEV) distribution. Secondly, we propose a novel lowerbound estimation method by using r-largest approach, by taking recent advances from EVT. We perform extensive experiments on four neural networks for three image datasets, including MNIST, Fashion MNIST and CIFAR10. We compare our lower bound estimation method with CLEVER as well as GEV, which demonstrate that our method significantly outperforms the state-of-the-art method, CLEVER, with around 10%âˆ’40% improvement. We also implement the adversarial attack method, C&W attack,to verify the correctness of the lower bound, by showing that the upper bound obtained from C&W attack is consistently larger than the lower bond from our method. Also, based on the lower bound of robustness, we observed that batch normalized CNN without drop-out technique yields a more robust performance than other neural network structures in our experiments.
